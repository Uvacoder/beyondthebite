Doctors on the Night Shift at Ola During Children’s Hospital in Freetown are on the frontline of the fight against malaria. Sierra Leone has one of the world’s highest rates of malaria and young children are particularly vulnerable.

The Car Wash Boys

by Amjata Bayoh
PLAY VIDEO

The car wash boys work outside throughout the night. Exposed to mosquitoes, they get malaria regularly. But if they don’t work, they won’t get the money they rely on to feed themselves and their families.

It threatens our mothers…

The Riskiest Place to Give Birth

by Elizabeth Katta
PLAY VIDEO

Sierra Leone is the riskiest places in the world to give birth and malaria increases the risk of death during pregnancy and childbirth. Elizabeth Katta had malaria four times during her pregnancy and gave birth in the midst of a malaria fever.

...and our children

A Birthday Commemoration

by Emmanuel Bundu
PLAY VIDEO

At a birthday celebration, Emmanuel commemorates his son Shadrach who died of malaria last year. It’s Shadrach’s first birthday since his death and Emmanuel is determined not to spend it on his own.

It breeds in our communities...

A Clean Sweep

by Mohamed Kamara
PLAY VIDEO

Monthly mandatory cleaning days are part of a national effort to get rid of rubbish and stop stagnant pools of water forming in blocked gutters, where mosquitoes breed. However, many of Freetown’s slum communities face an almost-impossible struggle to keep their community clean. Mohamed Kamara meets the Mayor of Freetown to find out about her plans to improve the city’s sanitation systems.

About

People say if you haven’t had malaria, you’re not a true Sierra Leonean.

We are a network of citizen reporters in Sierra Leone who have spent the last year gathering stories from people affected by malaria in all walks of life.

These films chart how the stories of our lives here - of school days, love, work and parenthood - are wrapped up in the story of this dangerous killer.


For more stories from our network, visit: onourradar.org/malaria
